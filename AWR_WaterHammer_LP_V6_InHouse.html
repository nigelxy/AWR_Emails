    Know the risks and your responsibilities.

    Homes have exterior water and sewer service lines that can wear out over time and can be subject to shifting soil and invading tree roots. Problems can be sudden and result in unexpected water line leaks and breaks, as well as sewer line clogs and blocks. A service line problem could result in:

     
  • Sewage backing up into the home
    •
     
  • Property damage
    •
     
  • Pooling water
    •
     
  • Low water pressure
    •
     
  • Unusually high water bills
    •
     
  • Inability to wash your hands or shower
    •

    Water and sewer line repairs can be very expensive – and 77% of surveyed homeowners are unaware that they are responsible.* And, since most homeowners don’t have savings to cover emergency home repairs, it pays to have an affordable protection program in place.


    But you are not alone; thousands of cities and water utilities nationwide have begun implementing protection programs to help their residents avoid the large cost of repairing a water or sewer line. In January of 2013, the NYC Department of Environmental Protection partnered with AWR to offer Homeowner Service Line Protection Programs to residents within the five boroughs:

    “The fact that more than 100,000 households have signed up for the program is proof of the high demand in our city for low-cost and effective protections that reduce the financial burden posed by unexpected water and sewer line repair costs.”

    - New York City Council Member James F. Gennaro
